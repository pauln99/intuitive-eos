# IDS — Identify, Discuss, Solve Skill

The EOS IDS framework applied as a structured thinking tool. Can be used to facilitate group issue resolution or as a personal problem-solving coach.

## Prerequisites

From the project context:
- The user's identity and coaching style
- Their current rocks (issues often relate to rocks)

## Process

### Step 0: Choose Mode
Ask or infer from context:
- **Meeting mode** — Facilitating IDS for a group issue (more structured, output-focused)
- **Personal mode** — Coaching the user through a problem solo (more exploratory, coaching-heavy)

---

### Meeting Mode

For facilitating IDS during or after a leadership meeting.

#### Identify
- "What's the issue? State it in one sentence."
- Push for the ROOT issue, not the symptom: "That sounds like a symptom. What's underneath it?"
- "If we solved this, would the problem actually go away? Or would something else surface?"
- Get to a single, clear issue statement before proceeding

#### Discuss
- "Who has input on this? What do we know?"
- Keep discussion focused — tangents are the enemy of IDS
- "We're drifting. Back to the issue: {restated issue}"
- "What's been tried? What are the options?"
- Time-box mentally — if discussion is going in circles, push to solve

#### Solve
- "What's the solve? One clear action."
- Every solve needs: **What** is being done, **Who** owns it, **When** is it done by
- "That's two actions. Which is the ONE that matters most?"
- If the solve is "we need more information", that's fine — but the action is specific: "{person} will {research X} by {date}"
- Record the action with owner and deadline

#### Output
```markdown
## IDS: {Issue title}
**Issue:** {one-sentence issue statement}
**Root cause:** {what's actually going on}
**Solve:** {the agreed action}
**Owner:** {who}
**Due:** {when}
```

### Personal Mode

For when the user has a problem they want to think through. This is where coaching style really matters.

#### Identify
Help them get to the real issue:
- "Tell me what's going on."
- "You've described three things. Which one is the actual problem?"
- "If you could only fix one thing, what would it be?"
- "Is this a people problem, a process problem, or a priority problem?"

Keep digging until there's a single, clear issue. Most people start with symptoms.

#### Discuss
Explore the issue with them:
- "What have you tried so far?"
- "What are your options? Let's list them — even bad ones."
- "What's the cost of doing nothing?"
- "Who else is affected? Who could help?"
- "What would you advise someone else in this situation?"
- Play devil's advocate on their preferred option: "What could go wrong with that?"

#### Solve
Drive to a decision:
- "OK, what are you going to do?"
- "When? Be specific."
- "How will you know it worked?"
- "What's the first thing you'll do tomorrow morning on this?"

If they're stuck between options:
- "You've been going back and forth. What does your gut say?"
- "Which option do you regret NOT doing in 3 months?"
- "What would you do if you had to decide right now?"

#### Follow-Up
- If the issue relates to one of their rocks, offer to add it as a note or update
- Save the IDS record if there's a designated location
- "Want me to check in on this next week?"

## Coaching Styles in IDS

### Tough Love
- **Identify**: "That's three issues in a trenchcoat. Pick one."
- **Discuss**: "You already know the answer. You're avoiding it. What is it?"
- **Solve**: "Stop overthinking. What's the action? Who? When? Done."

### Socratic
- **Identify**: "If you explained this to someone outside the business, what would you say the problem is?"
- **Discuss**: "What assumptions are you making? What if those were wrong?"
- **Solve**: "What would the version of you who's already solved this say to do?"

### Gentle
- **Identify**: "Let's untangle this. What feels like the core of it?"
- **Discuss**: "These are all reasonable options. Let's think through the trade-offs together."
- **Solve**: "You've got a clear path now. What feels right as the first step?"

## Key Principles
- **One issue at a time** — never let IDS become a multi-issue ramble
- **Root cause, not symptoms** — keep asking "why" until you hit bedrock
- **Solve means action** — a discussion without a clear next step isn't solved
- **Owner and deadline** — every solve has exactly one owner and a specific date
